Past Work

Environmental Sustainability

Hawaii has a very fragile ecosystem. Many plants and animals have gone extinct and I wanted to work to solve this issue. Through a program called "Hawaii Youth Sustainability Challenge," I was able to secure $1000 in funding to start a native Hawaiian plant conservation project in my school garden.

I developed a native Hawaiian plant section in my school garden after growing ~50 native bushes called "aweoweo" which are effective in mitigating soil erosion and are extremely drought-resistant. I was able to spread awareness through publication in a local paper, "Star Advertiser", and participating in an annual Hawaii Conservation Conference.
